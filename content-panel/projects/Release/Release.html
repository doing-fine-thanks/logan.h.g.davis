Release

(2017)

Statement:

Release is an attempt to convey my personal difficulties with reading across intersubjective boundaries. Salom√© Voegelin outlines the practice and principles of expressing "sonic possible worlds"; Release tried to create a Textual Possible Worlds. Though not an objective recreation of my difficulties with text, the caricature tries to more forcefully place the reader in an adversarial relationship with the act of reading the work.

I've always had issues with manufacturing confidence in my feelings so that they can live on a page and maintain some semblance of direct writing. I am a person, my feelings change and my opinions are sometimes contradictory. Using the self altering nature of the essay to also encode multiple versions of each essay in the work. The layering of these different, sometimes contradictory, versions of memories, opinions, and feelings help construct a much more accurate viewpoint of the Possible Textual World I am conveying. The generated collection, though large, is far more bound than something like Jorge Luis Borges's Library of Babel as it's still limited to personally written fragments (instead of the uncurated random permutations).
